🧠 Core Concepts Explained

What is a Merit Score? ▼
Your merit score is a single number calculated from your MBBS result, house job, and other factors as per the current PRP policy. Higher is better. All candidates are ranked by this score, and those with higher scores get first pick.
Example: A score of 32.5 is better than 28.5. The candidate with 32.5 will be offered seats first.
What is Closing Merit? ▼
The closing merit (or cutoff) is the lowest score that got a seat in a particular specialty and hospital in a given year. If your score is above the closing merit, you would have gotten that seat.
Example: Closing merit = 27.2. If your score is 28.5, you're above the cutoff and would likely qualify.
What do ✅ Likely / ⚡ Close Call / ❌ Difficult mean? ▼
These are outcome predictions based on comparing your score to the closing merit:

✅ Very Likely — Your score is comfortably above the cutoff (by more than 1 point).
⚡ Close Call — You're within the borderline range. You might get it depending on the year.
❌ Difficult — The cutoff is significantly above your score. Unlikely but not impossible if it drops.
What is Percentile? ▼
Percentile tells you what percentage of all hospital closing merits are below your score. A higher percentile means more options are open to you.
Example: 70th percentile → your score beats the cutoff for 70% of all specialty-hospital combinations.
What is Volatility? ▼
Volatility measures how much the cutoff jumps around from year to year.

Low volatility = very predictable. The cutoff barely changes.
High volatility = unpredictable. Could be 35 one year and 26 the next.
Tip: Prefer low-volatility specialties if you want a safe, predictable outcome.
What is Prediction Confidence? ▼
Confidence tells you how reliable the prediction is. It's based on how many years of data are available for that specialty-hospital pair.

High confidence = 4+ years of data. Very reliable.
Low confidence = only 1–2 years. Take the prediction with a grain of salt.
What does Trend mean (Rising / Falling / Stable)? ▼
Trend shows which direction the cutoff has been moving in recent years.

Falling = cutoffs are going down → getting easier. Good for you if you're borderline.
Rising = cutoffs are going up → getting harder.
Stable = cutoffs are relatively unchanged.
What is Competitiveness? ▼
Competitiveness is based on the average closing merit for a specialty. Higher average closing merit = more competitive.

Very High = typically requires a very strong score.
Low = most applicants can get in.

💡 Tips for Applicants

Use the Strategy Tool first It gives you a ready-made safe/moderate/ambitious list to guide your actual application choices.
Don't ignore borderline options If a specialty has a falling trend and low volatility, a Close Call might actually work out.
Check multiple years of data One unusual year can skew the average. Look at the trend chart for the full picture.
High volatility = higher risk A specialty that was easy last year might become hard this year. Treat it as uncertain.
Prefer high-confidence predictions Low-confidence predictions are based on limited data. Weight them less in your planning.
Filter by your program and quota Merits differ significantly between Punjab/Open and other quotas. Always filter to your own quota.
⚠️ Important Disclaimer: All predictions on this platform are based on historical official merit list data only. Past cutoffs do not guarantee future results. Seat availability, policy changes, and candidate pool size change every year. Always verify with official PHF / PGMI sources before making any application decisions. This tool is for planning and awareness only — not a substitute for official guidance.
