Why Does This Cost Money Now?

Until recently, PRP merit list data was openly accessible — no barriers, no restrictions. That changed when PRPData Stat gained traction among candidates who used it to hold committees accountable for seat manipulation and backroom allocations.

In what many see as a deliberate move to limit public oversight, the endpoints were progressively rate-limited, CAPTCHAed, and geo-blocked — making direct access impossible without infrastructure. Running this now requires rotating proxies and cloud VMs just to fetch the same data that was completely free to read before.

This isn't a technical problem. It's a political one.
